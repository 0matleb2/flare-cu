## Slide 2 — The Problem

**Concordia SGW is an open urban campus. Conditions change fast.**

- Protests, blockades, dense crowds, construction, entrance closures
- Google Maps optimizes for speed — not safety, accessibility, or calm
- Official alerts (email, Rave Guardian) inform but don't guide navigation
- Students are left to interpret fragmented info under stress

**Flare CU** gives students one calm place to see what's happening, judge how credible it is, and decide what to do next.

## Slide 3 — Our Users

Three primary user groups, each with specific constraints:

**Mobility-constrained students**
- Need barrier-free routes, ramps, elevators
- Detours during disruptions may be inaccessible

**Anxiety / sensory-sensitive students**
- Benefit from reduced cognitive load
- Need calm language, minimal stimulation

**Students new to campus**
- Unfamiliar with building layouts and alternate routes
- Need clear, contextual guidance

## Slide 7 — 10 Usability Heuristics

| # | Heuristic | How Flare CU applies it |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Visibility of system status | Online/offline badge, credibility labels, progress bars |
| 2 | Match between system and real world | "Flare" not "alert," calm familiar language |
| 3 | User control and freedom | Undo after report, exit emergency anytime |
| 4 | Consistency and standards | Material Design 3, consistent card layout and colors |
| 5 | Error prevention | Structured categories, confirmation before emergency exit |
| 6 | Recognition rather than recall | Sort chips, category chips, location chips, route cards |
| 7 | Flexibility and efficiency | Guest mode, sort options, alert intensity levels |
| 8 | Aesthetic and minimalist design | Calm Tech, emergency strips to 3 tabs, low-stim mode |
| 9 | Help users recover from errors | Undo snackbar, offline queues and syncs |
| 10 | Help and documentation | Quick Setup explains options, settings describe effects |
