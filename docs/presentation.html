The Problem

Concordia SGW is an open urban campus. Conditions change fast.

  • Protests, blockades, dense crowds, construction, entrance closures
  • Google Maps optimizes for speed — not safety, accessibility, or calm
  • Official alerts (email, Rave Guardian) inform but don't guide navigation
  • Students must interpret fragmented info under stress and time pressure

Flare CU gives students one calm place to see what's happening nearby, judge how credible it is, and decide what to do next.

Our Users

Mobility-constrained

Need barrier-free routes, ramps, elevators. Detours during disruptions may be inaccessible.

Anxiety / sensory-sensitive

Benefit from reduced cognitive load. Need calm language, minimal stimulation.

New to campus

Unfamiliar with layouts and alternate routes. Need clear, contextual guidance.

Double Diamond Design Process

Discover

Reviewed Concordia safety pages, campus maps, accessibility resources, public disruption reports

Define

Built personas, empathy maps, journey maps; specified 10 user goals from real constraints

Develop

Low-fi wireframes for layout and flow, then iterated into high-fi prototype

Deliver

Functional prototype on real device, evaluated against 10 usability heuristics

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

Grayscale wireframes established screen hierarchy, navigation structure, and information layout before visual design.

Low-Fi → High-Fi Evolution

Core structure stayed the same. High-fi added Concordia color system, real interaction states, and accessibility adaptations.

10 Usability Heuristics

#HeuristicHow Flare CU applies it
1Visibility of system statusOnline/offline badge, credibility labels, progress bars
2Match between system and real world"Flare" not "alert," calm familiar language
3User control and freedomUndo after report, exit emergency anytime, "Are you safe?"
4Consistency and standardsMaterial Design 3, consistent card layout and credibility colors
5Error preventionStructured categories prevent vague reports, confirm before emergency exit
6Recognition rather than recallSort chips, category chips, location chips, route option cards
7Flexibility and efficiency of useGuest mode shortcut, sort options, alert intensity levels
8Aesthetic and minimalist designCalm Tech, emergency strips to 3 tabs, low-stim mode
9Help users recover from errorsUndo snackbar, offline queues reports and syncs later
10Help and documentationQuick Setup explains options, settings describe effects in plain language
